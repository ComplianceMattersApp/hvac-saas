Status: ACTIVE content lock
Purpose: keep onboarding and trial copy consistent so new companies know exactly what to do first.

Audience:

Language rules for user-facing copy:
- Use plain English and short steps

- Prefer: Start here. Do this first. This can wait. Create your first job. Schedule the job. Use Today/Ops each morning.

## 1) 14-Day Trial Success Path

### First login / Day 0

What to do:
- Start in setup and company profile
- Confirm company name and support contact info
- Invite your core team (office and at least one tech)

Why it matters:
- Your team needs correct basics before daily work starts

Where to go:
- Admin Center
- Company Profile
- Internal Users

What success looks like:
- Company info is correct
- Team invites are sent

What can wait:
- Advanced settings and optional tools

### Day 1

What to do:
- Create your first customer
- Create your first job

Why it matters:
- Trial value starts when real work is entered

Where to go:
- Customers
- Jobs
- New Job

What success looks like:
- One real customer and one real job are in the system

What can wait:
- Deep reports and advanced setup

### Days 2-3

What to do:
- Schedule jobs
- Assign jobs to technicians
- Use Today/Ops each morning

Why it matters:
- Clear scheduling and assignment prevents dropped work

Where to go:
- Today
- Ops queues

What success looks like:
- Scheduled jobs are assigned
- The team starts each day from Today/Ops

What can wait:
- Optional extras not needed for this week

### Days 4-7

What to do:
- Have techs use the job page during field work
- Capture notes and key work details
- Move finished work to closeout

Why it matters:
- Good field notes make office follow-up and invoicing easier

Where to go:
- Job page
- Ops closeout queue

What success looks like:
- Field work details are captured
- Jobs move cleanly into closeout

What can wait:
- Advanced analytics and deep optimization

### Days 8-14

What to do:
- Keep the daily Today/Ops rhythm
- Finish closeout and handle invoices consistently

Why it matters:
- A repeatable daily routine is the core trial outcome

Where to go:
- Today
- Ops
- Closeout queue
- Invoice pages

What success looks like:
- Work is moving from field completion to invoice without confusion

What can wait:
- Features not required for daily execution

### End-of-trial check-in

What to do:
- Review results with owner and core team

Why it matters:
- Decide based on real daily use, not feature count

Where to go:
- Today
- Ops
- Jobs
- Closeout
- Invoice pages

What success looks like:
- Team can run real work from customer to invoice

What can wait:
- Everything that did not block daily work in trial

## 2) First Week Success Checklist

Use this checklist in week one:
- Company info confirmed
- Team and techs invited
- First customer created
- First job created
- Job scheduled and assigned
- Field notes and work details captured
- Work moved to closeout and invoice handled
- Today/Ops used each morning and before end of day

Suggested in-app checklist wording:
- Start here: confirm company details
- Invite your team
- Create your first customer
- Create your first job
- Schedule and assign the job
- Capture field notes
- Close out the work and handle the invoice
- Use Today/Ops each morning

## 3) Owner-Led Walkthrough Script

Use this on onboarding calls:

1. Start here
- "Today we are not trying to set up everything. We are going to run one real job end to end."

2. Setup and company profile
- "First, confirm company details so everyone sees the right business info."

3. Invite team
- "Invite office/dispatch and one tech. That is enough to start."

4. Create first customer and first job
- "Now create one real customer and one real job."

5. Schedule and assign
- "Put the job on the calendar and assign it so the board is clear."

6. Field job page
- "Have the tech open the job and add notes while the work is happening."

7. Closeout and invoice
- "When work is done, move it to closeout and handle the invoice step."

8. Daily routine
- "Each morning, use Today/Ops to see what needs attention first."

9. Set expectations
- "If this daily flow is working by the end of trial, you are on track."

## 4) Role-Based Quick Starts

### Owner/Admin
- What this role does first:
  - Confirm setup basics and team access
  - Ensure first real job runs end to end
- Page used most:
  - Admin Center, Today, Ops
- Can ignore at first:
  - Advanced settings and deep reports

### Dispatcher/Office
- What this role does first:
  - Keep jobs scheduled and assigned each day
- Page used most:
  - Today and Ops queues
- Can ignore at first:
  - Deep admin settings

### Technician
- What this role does first:
  - Open assigned jobs and capture field notes
- Page used most:
  - Today and Job page
- Can ignore at first:
  - Billing and admin pages

### Billing/AR
- What this role does first:
  - Keep closeout-to-invoice handoff moving
- Page used most:
  - Invoice pages and payments view
- Can ignore at first:
  - Advanced payment extras

### ECC/Rater
- What this role does first:
  - Keep testing and exception follow-up moving
- Page used most:
  - Today, Ops exceptions, Job page
- Can ignore at first:
  - Service-only extras not needed for current jobs

## 5) What to Ignore at First

Give users permission to skip these in trial:
- Advanced reports
- Service plans (unless needed now)
- Payment automation extras
- Contractor collaboration tools if not used now
- Deep settings clean-up
- Optional app/device setup items unless needed

Focus first on daily work execution.

## 6) Trial Success Criteria

Core proof point:
- A company can run real work from customer -> job -> schedule -> field notes -> closeout -> invoice.

End-of-trial checks:
- Real jobs were created
- Work was scheduled and assigned
- Techs used the job page
- Notes and work details were captured
- Closeout and invoices were handled
- Team used Today/Ops to see what needed attention

## 7) 14-Day Trial Copy Rules

- Runtime trial is 30 days
- Public and user-facing copy should clearly say "30-day trial"
- Standard new-account trial policy updated from 15 days to 30 days for newly created trial entitlements. Existing entitlement truth is not retroactively changed by code; the one known active trial was extended separately by controlled operator action.
- This slice does not change runtime trial logic

Suggested user-facing copy lines:
- "Start your 30-day trial"
- "Your 30-day trial ends on [date]"
- "In this 30-day trial, focus on daily job flow first"
